About Me

I am a second-year Electrical Engineering student at the University of Texas at El Paso (UTEP) with a 3.94 GPA, where I conduct research in the Power & Renewable Energy Systems (PRES) Lab. Since my second semester, I have focused on applying machine learning and data-driven methods to modern power system challenges, including renewable energy forecasting and grid resilience.

I previously interned at Lawrence Livermore National Laboratory (LLNL) as a Power Systems Intern and have accepted a return offer for Summer 2026. My work spans both academic research and national lab applications, bridging theory with real-world energy systems.

At UTEP, I have served as a lead (first) author on multiple peer-reviewed papers accepted to IEEE conferences, with research covering probabilistic load forecasting, and LLMs for grid operations, and, most recently, data centers as virtual power plants. I have also collaborated with industry partners such as Eaton, contributing to research on transformer monitoring and diagnostics.

My broader interests lie in power systems, renewable energy integration, and the role of AI in modernizing the electrical grid. I am particularly motivated by large-scale challenges in energy reliability, sustainability, and efficiency. Beyond engineering, I hold strong interests in economics, philosophy, politics, and history, which inform my perspective on complex systems and long-term societal challenges.
